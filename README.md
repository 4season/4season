Hello, I’m Junho Heo

I am an undergraduate student in Computer Science interested in computational neuroscience, human learning, decision-making, and computational psychiatry.

My academic interests began with a simple question: why do people process the same information differently, learn at different speeds, and make different decisions under similar conditions? I hope to approach this question by combining computational models, behavioral data, and neural data.

Research Interests

* Computational neuroscience
* Computational psychiatry
* Reinforcement learning and decision-making
* Human learning and cognitive modeling
* Behavioral and neural data analysis

Current Focus

I have experience in data preprocessing, machine learning model implementation, and backend development through projects involving MLPs, CNNs, structured and unstructured data, and API-based systems.

I am currently building a stronger foundation in reinforcement learning, probabilistic modeling, neuroscience, and computational analysis of behavioral and neural data. My long-term goal is to study how attention, arousal, learning, and decision-making can be modeled computationally in both healthy and clinical populations.

Selected Projects

* ConvDDI — CNN-based drug–drug interaction prediction
* TrendXiv — Analysis and prediction of arXiv research trends
* Bus Delay Prediction — MLP-based prediction of bus arrival delays
* Magnolia — Backend and notification-processing system
